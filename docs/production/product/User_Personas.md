# User Personas

To ensure product-market fit, all UX and game design decisions in Lerno are driven by the following core personas:

## 1. The Achiever (Age 8-12)

- **Motivations**: Collecting rare badges, reaching the top of the Diamond League, showing off an exclusive Avatar.
- **Behaviors**: Will grind through math and science lessons purely to earn Coins to buy cosmetics. Highly competitive in 1v1 battles.
- **Pain Points**: Gets bored easily by static multiple-choice questions. Needs flashing animations, sounds, and tangible rewards to stay focused.
- **Lerno's Solution**: The XP and Coin economy, public profiles, and tiered League systems.

## 2. The Explorer (Age 6-9)

- **Motivations**: Unlocking new map areas, seeing what happens when they complete a learning path, playing the Flame mini-games.
- **Behaviors**: Prefers the solo Learning Path over the competitive 1v1 battles. Enjoys the narrative and the journey.
- **Pain Points**: Gets discouraged by overly punishing failure states.
- **Lerno's Solution**: Zero-punishment learning loops, interactive drag-and-drop Flame games, and constant positive reinforcement (celebration animations).

## 3. The Parent/Guardian

- **Motivations**: Ensuring their child is actually learning and not just wasting time on a screen.
- **Behaviors**: Reviews the child's profile to see completed subjects and total stars.
- **Pain Points**: Struggles to force the child to do homework.
- **Lerno's Solution**: Disguising rigorous syllabus content within a hyper-casual game interface. (Future integration: Parent Dashboard for detailed analytics).
